Moroccan Shepherds Pie

Sweet Potato Mash

  • 1.5 lb sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp milk

Filling

  • 1 lb ground lamb or beef
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp ginger root
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 (28 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup currants or raisins

Mash

  1. Boil large pot of water
  2. Boil potatoes for 15 minutes or until tender
  3. Drain well and add olive oil, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and milk
  4. Mash until creamy and set aside

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 350 F
  2. Brown meat in skillet over medium heat
  3. Remove meat and set aside
  4. Add oil with onions, garlic, ginger, salt, and spices and cook until onions are softened
  5. Return meat to pan and add honey, tomatoes, and stock
  6. Bring to boil and simmer until sauce is thickened, 10 min or more
  7. Add chickpeas and currants/raisins. Stir and remove from heat. Season with salt and pepper to taste

Assembly

  1. Fill baking dish with filling and spread mash on top.
  2. Bake for 40 minutes until top is beginning to brown

Lentils and Rice

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 4 medium cloves garlic, smashed and peeled
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 ¾ teaspoons fine sea salt, divided
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 cups water
  • 1 cup white basmati rice (regular, not quick-cooking), rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup regular brown or green lentils, picked over for debris, rinsed and drained
  • ⅓ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 medium-to-large yellow onions, halved and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup thinly sliced green onions (from 1 bunch), divided

Recipe

  1. In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, combine the garlic, bay leaves, cumin, 1 ½ teaspoons of the salt and about 20 twists of freshly ground black pepper. Add the water and bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Cook the lentils in the boiling water first for about 10 minutes (until somewhat softened on the outside/still firm in the middle), then add the rice and cook until both lentils and rice are tender, about 25 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, warm the olive oil in a large (12-inch) skillet over medium-high heat. When it’s warm enough that a slice of onion sizzles on contact, add the remaining onions. Stir to combine.
  4. Stir only every 3 minutes or so at first, then more often once the onions at the edges of the pan start browning. If the onions are browning before they have softened, dial down the heat to give them more time. Cook until the onions are deeply caramelized and starting to crisp at the edges, about 20 to 30 minutes. In the meantime, line a large plate or cutting board with a couple paper towels.
  5. Using a slotted spoon or fish spatula, transfer the onions to the lined plate and spread them evenly across. Sprinkle the remaining ¼ teaspoon salt over the onions. They’ll crisp up as they cool.
  6. When the lentils and rice are done cooking, drain off any excess water (if there is any) and return the mixture to the pot, off the heat. Lay a kitchen towel across the top of the pot to absorb steam, then cover the pot and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  7. Remove the lid, discard the bay leaves, and smash the garlic cloves against the side of the pan with a fork. Add about ¾ths of the green onions, reserving the rest for garnish. Gently stir and fluff the rice with a fork. Season to taste with additional salt and pepper, if necessary.
  8. Transfer the rice and lentil mixture to a large serving platter or bowl. Top with the caramelized onions and the remaining green onions. Serve hot, warm or at room temperature, with yogurt and spicy sauce (optional) on the side.

Venison Meatballs

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground venison
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1/2 lb ground pork or bacon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • Oil

Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 375 F.
  2. Heat oil in skillet on medium. Add onions and sauté for 5 minutes. Add garlic, red wine vinegar, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Cook until lightly carmelized.
  3. Add mixture to ground venison, pork, egg, and breadcrumbs in mixing bowl. Mix until just combined.
  4. Using ice cream scoop, form meatballs and place on baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes.

Spam Fried Rice with Corn and Shishito Peppers

SUB1

  • 4 cups cooked white rice (12oz)
  • 4 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 can spam, finely diced
  • 12 oz fresh corn kernels (2 cobs)
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 scallions, sliced, whites and greens separated or half an onion
  • 24 shishito peppers, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp light soy
  • 2 tsp roasted sesame oil

Recipe

  1. Store day old rice loosely covered in fridge.
  2. If using fresh rice, rinse well before cooking and once cooked, transfer to rimmed baking sheet in front of a fan for 1 hour.
  3. Break up rice into individual grains in medium bowl
  4. Heat wok over high heat and add 1.5 tsp of oil. Add spam and stir fry until crisp. Transfer spam to bowl, leaving oil behind.
  5. Return wok to high heat. Add corn and cook until lightly charred. Transfer to spam bowl.
  6. Return wok to high heat. Add another 1.5 tsp oil. Add scallion whites and shishito peppers, stir fry until lightly softened and fragrant. Transfer to bowl with spam and corn.
  7. Return wok to high heat. Add remaining oil and add rice and cook until pale brown. Use blowtorch to add wok hei flavor by pointing torch directly at food 4-6 inches away while tossing. You want the tiny droplets of oil to crackle and pop.
  8. Return corn, spam, and pepper mixture to wok. Make a space to add a little more oil. Add egg, scramble, and combine with rest of ingredients. Add soy sauce and sesame oil and toss. Season with salt to taste. Add scallion greens and toss. Serve immediately.

Crisp Gnocchi and Brussels Sprouts with Brown Butter

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 16 oz Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1/2 tsp lemon zest
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 16 oz gnocchi
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • splash of balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp honey
  • Parmesan, grated

Recipe

  1. Heat cast iron pan on medium.
  2. Add 3 tbsp oil, then brussels sprouts in an even layer and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook for 10-12 minutes and add lemon zest.
  4. Continue cooking sprouts until browned on both sides.
  5. Remove sprouts from pan and set aside.
  6. Add 1 tbsp oil to pan and add gnocchi in single layer.
  7. Let cook without stirring, 3-4 minutes.
  8. Add butter and honey, cooking until golden brown and nutty.
  9. Add splash of balsamic.
  10. Stir sprouts back in and cook until mixed thoroughly.
  11. Serve with parmesan cheese grated over the top.
